## Triggering Event Description

**What just happened (plain language):**

The National Weather Service has issued a TORNADO WARNING for Warren County. A confirmed tornado has been spotted approximately 12 miles southwest of Bowling Green and is tracking northeast directly toward the WKU campus. The warning is in effect for 45 minutes.

At the moment of issuance:
- L.T. Smith Stadium is at or near full capacity
- Tailgate activity is ongoing in all parking lots
- The sky has darkened noticeably; light rain has begun
- Tornado sirens in Bowling Green have activated
- WKU Alert has sent an initial text/email notification

## Current EOP Protocol in Effect [CONFIRM — verify against current WKU EOP]

**Protocol:** Tornado/Severe Weather Emergency — Mass Shelter-in-Place

**Expected actions per WKU EOP:**
1. WKU Alert text + email issued immediately
2. PA announcement in stadium by public address announcer
3. Campus police and event staff direct crowd to shelter locations
4. Outdoor tailgate areas cleared — people directed to stadium interior or nearest building
5. Interior shelter points activated: lowest level of stadium, DSU basement, garage structures
6. WKU Emergency Management coordinates with Warren County EMA
7. "All Clear" issued only by official WKU channels after NWS lifts warning

**Designated shelter points on game day:**
- Stadium interior (concourse level, below stands) — primary for 20,000+
- Downing Student Union basement
- Cravens Graduate Center lower levels
- Parking structure (Regents Ave Garage) — concrete structure
